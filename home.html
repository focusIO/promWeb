PANABO CITY

"Banana Capital Of the Philippines"

Panabo, officially the City of Panabo, is a component city in the province of Davao del Norte, Philippines. According to the 2020 census, it has a population of 209,230 people. It's neighboring cities are Tagum City and Davao City, along side municipalities of Carmen and Sto. Tomas.




Photos of Panabo City's Public Park, located in the Old City Hall, near Panabo City Grand Terminal.

CAGANGOHAN

Cagangohan is one of the forty barangays in the city of Panabo, in the province of Davao del Norte. Its population as determined by the 2020 Census was 13,224. This represented 6.32% of the total population of Panabo. It is also holds the home of the fisherfolks of the city and is the host of the Mariculture Center that serves the mariculture park innovation across the country.




Photos of Bureau of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources - National Mariculture Center, along with its Acclimatization Area and the Cagangohan Women's Organization located in Brgy. Cagangohan.



The Brgy. Cagangohan does not only hold the broodstock for the seafood provision of the city, but also caters rest to its citizens by its well-known Panabo City Seawall and Panabo City Mangrove Forest Park.
